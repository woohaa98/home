About Me

Twenty years of tech.
A lifetime of listening.

I grew up in Seoul and moved to the US in my early twenties — carrying with me a deep cultural rootedness and the disorienting, clarifying experience of starting over in a new world. That journey shaped everything: how I listen, what I notice, and why I care so much about the stories people carry inside them.

I've spent 20 years in tech as a Product Manager — building customer experience and data platforms, driving personalization, predictive modeling, and product-led growth. Now I'm training to become a Licensed Mental Health Counselor (LMHC), rooted in an existential-humanistic, narrative approach. I have deep, firsthand empathy for high-pressure professional environments — the always-on culture, identity wrapped up in performance, the quiet erosion of inner agency. And I understand intimately what it means to navigate between cultures, to translate not just language but whole ways of being, and how profoundly that shapes identity and belonging. I'm especially drawn to adults navigating identity questions, career transitions, and existential reevaluation — and committed to integrating AI into care thoughtfully and ethically, to extend the therapeutic relationship, never replace it.

Counseling Services

A space to reclaim
your own story.

Rooted in an existential-humanistic, narrative approach — I help adults reconnect with their inner agency, explore what truly matters, and author a life that feels authentically theirs. With 20 years inside the tech world, I understand the invisible pressures that make this hard.

01 High-Pressure Professionals

For adults navigating anxiety, stress, and burnout in demanding work environments. I bring firsthand understanding of performance-driven cultures — the pressure to perform, the cost of constant context-switching, the struggle to honor your inner life while meeting external expectations.

02 Identity & Existential Concerns

For those sitting with deeper questions — about meaning, values, purpose, and who they are beyond their role. Drawing on existential-humanistic and narrative approaches, we explore what you want your story to say, especially during periods of transition or disorientation.

03 Career Lifespan & Late-Stage Transitions

For professionals in later stages of their career — experiencing disengagement, reevaluating professional identity, or preparing for retirement. I offer a thoughtful, non-prescriptive space to process what's ending and what might be beginning.

04 Anxiety, Stress & Burnout

Grounded in an existential-humanistic lens, we work not just to manage symptoms but to understand what your anxiety or exhaustion is trying to tell you — and what it reveals about the values and pressures shaping your life.

My Approach

Existential-Humanistic.
Narrative. Somatic-Informed.

My primary theoretical foundation is existential-humanistic — I believe you are the author of your own story, and that real change comes from reconnecting with your capacity for choice, meaning, and agency. I integrate narrative therapy to help you examine the stories that have shaped you, and identify which ones are truly yours.

I'm particularly drawn to the intersection of inner life and outer pressure — how performance-driven cultures can quietly erode a person's sense of self, and how counseling can be a space to reclaim it. My 20 years in tech means I speak your language, and my commitment to Ethical Innovation in Counseling means I'm thoughtful about how AI can extend — never replace — the human relationship at the center of care.
